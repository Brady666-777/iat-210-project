# **Background story**

**Mass Effect: Ark Ascension**

The sun is dying.

Within a century, the life-giving star that nurtured countless civilizations will completely collapse, swallowing the entire galaxy in darkness and flame. Turians, Asari, Krogan, Salarians, and Humans—the five great species have laid down millennia of blood feuds to launch a final initiative: construct an Ark and escape the apocalypse.

But there are only enough resources to complete a single Ark.

Worse still, the dying sun remains greedily demanding. Periodically, it erupts with devastating tides of energy. Only by "feeding" the sun with the purest resources can this dying star be briefly pacified, buying precious time for construction. Every failed sacrifice brings doomsday one step closer. Three failures, and everyone perishes together.

You are the last hope of your species. Lead your people, mine the remaining metal, fuel, and crystals, and race to fill your Ark before your rivals do. Use strategy to slow down your competitors, trade to secure your survival, and do whatever it takes to stay alive.

Five civilizations. Only one can carry the spark of life forward.

When the stars finally go dark, only those aboard the Ark will live to witness a new dawn.

# **I. Game Setup**

### **Board Setup**

1. Place the **Galactic Central Board** in the center of the table.  
2. Categorize the **Resource Pools** (Metal, Fuel, Element Zero/Crystal) and place them beside the board.  
3. Shuffle the three card decks and place them face-down:  
   * **Action Deck** (Place in the "Event Zone")  
   * **Sabotage Deck** (Place in the "Black Market Zone")  
   * **Solar Penalty Deck** (Place in the "Solar Zone")  
4. Set the **Solar Sacrifice Marker** to the "0" position on the Cumulative Failure Track.

### **Player Setup**

1. Each player chooses a **Race Board** and takes the corresponding colored **Ship Miniature**.  
2. Starting Resources:  
   * All players receive: **3 Metal, 3 Fuel, 2 Crystals**  
   * Racial Bonuses:  
     | Race | Bonus Resources |  
     | :--- | :--- |  
     | **Turian** | \+1 Fuel |  
     | **Asari** | \+1 Crystal |  
     | **Krogan** | \+1 Metal |  
     | **Salarian** | \+1 Crystal |  
     | **Human** | \+1 Resource of choice |  
3. Starting Hand: Each player draws **2 Sabotage Cards**.  
4. All players place their ships at the **Starting Point: The Citadel**.  
5. Determine the starting player randomly (highest die roll recommended).

# ---

**II. Race Board Details**

Each board includes: Race Name/Icon, Passive Ability, Active Ability (with cost), and the **Ark Construction Track** (10 slots requiring specific resource combinations).

### **Turian: The Hierarchy Fleet**

* **Passive \[Tactical Discipline\]:** Movement fuel cost \-1 (Min. 1 fuel per move action).  
* **Active \[Covering Fire\]:** Spend 1 Fuel; gain \+2 to your die roll this turn (Valid for movement or mining).  
* **Ark Progress:** \* Slots 1-3: 2 Metal  
  * Slots 4-5: 2 Metal \+ 1 Fuel  
  * Slots 6-7: 2 Fuel  
  * Slots 8-9: 3 Metal  
  * Slot 10: 1 Metal \+ 2 Fuel \+ 3 Crystals

### **Asari: Guardians of Thessia**

* **Passive \[Biotic Resonance\]:** When drawing an Action Card, draw 2 and keep 1; discard the other.  
* **Active \[Enthrall\]:** Spend 1 Crystal; target an opponent to force a 1-for-1 resource swap (each player chooses which resource to give).  
* **Ark Progress:**  
  * Slots 1-3: 2 Crystals  
  * Slots 4-5: 2 Crystals \+ 1 Metal  
  * Slots 6-7: 2 Metal  
  * Slots 8-9: 3 Crystals  
  * Slot 10: 2 Crystals \+ 2 Fuel \+ 2 Metal

### **Krogan: Sons of Tuchanka**

* **Passive \[Blood Rage\]:** Once per turn, spend 1 Fuel to negate a Sabotage Card played against you.  
* **Active \[Hardcore Charge\]:** Spend 2 Fuel; move immediately to any unoccupied space on the board.  
* **Ark Progress:**  
  * Slots 1-4: 3 Metal  
  * Slots 5-6: 2 Metal \+ 2 Fuel  
  * Slots 7-8: 4 Metal  
  * Slot 9: 3 Fuel  
  * Slot 10: 3 Metal \+ 3 Fuel \+ 3 Crystals

### **Salarian: STG Tech**

* **Passive \[Research Acceleration\]:** Gain 1 Crystal automatically at the start of your turn.  
* **Active \[Intel Analysis\]:** Spend 1 Crystal; look at the top 3 cards of the Sabotage Deck. Reorder them or draw 1 into your hand.  
* **Ark Progress:**  
  * Slots 1-3: 2 Crystals  
  * Slots 4-5: 1 Crystal \+ 2 Metal  
  * Slots 6-7: 2 Fuel  
  * Slots 8-9: 3 Crystals  
  * Slot 10: 4 Crystals \+ 2 Metal \+ 2 Fuel

### **Human: Alliance Ark Program**

* **Passive \[Indomitable\]:** During Solar Sacrifice, if the goal is failed, any penalty you personally receive is halved (rounded up).  
* **Active \[Alliance Will\]:** Spend 1 resource of any type; your trades this turn cannot be sabotaged (hands are visible during trade).  
* **Ark Progress:**  
  * Slots 1-6: Alternating 2 Metal, 2 Fuel, or 2 Crystals  
  * Slots 7-9: Mixed resource pairs  
  * Slot 10: 2 Metal \+ 2 Fuel \+ 2 Crystals

**III. Galactic Central Board Design**

The board consists of **20 tiles** in a circular loop.

| Tile \# | Name | Type | Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 0 | **Citadel** | Start/Safe | No effect on stop; pass through to repair (discard 1 Sabotage card). |
| 1, 11, 16 | **Asteroid/Colony/Fortress** | Metal Mine | Roll die to mine Metal (Tile 16 gives 2x bonus). |
| 2, 10 | **Mass Relay** | Special | Spend 1 Fuel to warp to any other Mass Relay tile. |
| 3, 9, 14 | **Illium/Ruins/Temple** | Crystal Mine | Roll die to mine Crystals (Tile 14 gives 2x bonus). |
| 4 | **Omega Station** | Black Market | Draw 1 Sabotage card for free or buy 1 for 1 Crystal. |
| 5, 7, 12, 17 | **Hazard Zones** | Danger | Draw 1 Action Card immediately. |
| 6, 18 | **Wreck/Lab** | Fuel Depot | Roll die to collect Fuel (Tile 18 gives 2x bonus). |
| 8 | **Citadel Council** | Trading | Force a trade with any player; both roll, winner sets terms. |
| 15 | **Solar Observatory** | Special | Peek at the top Solar Penalty card; choose whether to trigger it now. |
| 19 | **Ark Drydock** | Finish/Safe | Stop here to build 1 Ark Slot for free (Limit: once per game). |

**IV. Card Lists (Selected Examples)**

### **Action Deck (30 Cards)**

* **Positive:** *Resource Abundance* (Gain 1 of each), *Normandy Support* (Gain 2 Fuel \+ move 1 space).  
* **Negative:** *Engine Failure* (Cannot move this turn), *Reaper Scout* (Roll: 1-3 lose 2 Crystals; 4-6 safe).  
* **Event:** *Citadel Meeting* (Players vote on who gets 2 free resources).

### **Sabotage Deck (20 Cards)**

* **EMP:** Target loses 2 Fuel.  
* **Hack:** Target discards a Sabotage card or loses 1 Crystal.  
* **Sabotage Protocol:** Target loses 1 completed Ark Slot (must rebuild).  
* **Phantom Directive:** Discard 2 cards to control an opponent's next move direction.

### **Solar Penalty Deck (10 Cards)**

* **Solar Flare:** All players lose 2 of their most abundant resource.  
* **Time Warp:** Hand limit reduced by 2 for two turns.  
* **Final Countdown:** Cumulative failure count \+1.

# ---

**V. Turn Sequence**

1. **Step 0: Start of Turn:** Salarians gain 1 Crystal.  
2. **Step 1: Solar Sacrifice (All Players):** Active player rolls 2 dice for the "Sacrifice Value." All players contribute resources to the pool. If total \< Value, trigger a **Solar Penalty** and advance the failure track. **3 failures \= Game Over for everyone.**  
3. **Step 2: Movement:** Spend 1 Fuel. Roll 1 die. Move clockwise. If landing on a space with another player, pay them 1 resource or draw an Action card.  
4. **Step 3: Tile Resolution:** Execute the effect of the space landed upon (Mining, Danger, etc.).  
5. **Step 4: Action Phase:** \* Use 1 Race Ability.  
   * Play 1 Sabotage Card.  
   * Trade with others.  
   * **Build Ark:** Pay resources to fill slots in order.  
6. **Step 5: End of Turn:** Check hand limit (7) and pass to the left.

# ---

**VI. Winning Condition**

The first player to **fill all 10 Ark Slots** on their board wins the game and saves their species from the supernova.

**Test Conclusions & Balance Feedback**

1. **Distinct Racial Playstyles:**  
   * **Krogans** act as "risk investors." Though their slot costs are high, their explosive mining potential and teleportation ability make them massive late-game threats.  
   * **Turians** boast a smooth early-game advantage due to their highly efficient movement, allowing them to capture resource points quickly.  
2. **The "Solar Sacrifice" Psychological Game:**  
   * Playtesting revealed that when the Sacrifice Value ($N \> 8$), a distinct "free-rider" problem emerges, where players try to bluff and force others to pay more.  
   * The **Human** passive ability provides excellent error tolerance when a sacrifice fails, allowing them to remain competitive even during resource droughts.  
3. **Interaction Intensity:**  
   * Because landing on a tile occupied by an opponent requires paying a toll, and due to the constant threat of Sabotage cards, players are forced to carefully calculate their movement steps to avoid each other, adding a deep layer of spatial strategy.
